Valkyrie

// Waifu for the T-Watch S3

Valkyrie turns a LilyGO T-Watch S3 into a tiny BLE and Wi-Fi watchdog. It spots stuff like trackers, Flipper Zero, card skimmers, Flock cameras and drone fingerprints, plays along with Meshtastic mesh life, then makes the whole thing feel like a little RPG on your wrist. Plug it in, hit flash and let the magic smoke stay inside the watch. She scan, she exp, but most of all, she protecc.

Your browser can't flash over USB. Open this page in desktop Chrome, Edge or Opera (Web Serial is not available in Safari or Firefox). Flashing needs a secure connection. Open this page over https:// (or http://localhost). Quick start

Use desktop Chrome, Edge or Opera. Connect the watch with a USB data cable, click Flash Valkyrie and pick the serial port. The ESP Web Tools dialog also has Logs & Console when you want to see what the watch is saying.

Get started

Flash it without the drama

No PlatformIO, no terminal arcana, no “which Python is this?” side quest. Just a browser, a cable and the watch.

Plug in the watch

Connect your T-Watch S3 with a USB data cable. Different watch versions use different connectors, so the only real rule is: make sure the cable can do data, not just charging.

Click Flash Valkyrie

Open this page in desktop Chrome, Edge or Opera, then smash the Flash Valkyrie button up top.

Pick the serial port

Choose the watch from the browser popup. If it does not show up, try another port, another cable or unplug and plug it back in.

Wait for the install

Let it finish before touching anything. Once it reboots, use Logs & Console in the same dialog if you want to watch the waifu boot up.

Get your data

Pull your wardrive logs

Wardrive mode saves Wigle 1.6 CSVs to /valkyrie/wardrive/ on the watch. Plug in over USB and pull them straight into your browser — no app, no CLI. Files download to your normal Downloads folder, ready for Wigle.net.

This browser can't talk to the watch over USB. Use desktop Chrome, Edge or Opera (Web Serial isn't available in Safari or Firefox), served over https:// or http://localhost.

What it does

          
What you get on the watch

          

            Valkyrie uses the radios already in the T-Watch S3. BLE runs on
            NimBLE, Wi-Fi hops the common channels and the scan cycle stays
            power-aware so the watch is not melting itself for sport.
          


          

            

              

                
              

              
BLE threat scanning

              

                Watches for AirTag / Find My trackers, Flipper Zero, HC-03/05/06
                skimmers, Flock cameras, Meta Ray-Ban / Quest gear and BLE
                RemoteID. AirTags get extra checks so one random ping does not
                become panic theater.
              

              

                AirTag
                Flipper
                Skimmers
                Meta
              

            


            

              

                
              

              
Wi-Fi threat pass

              

                Hops channels 1, 6 and 11 looking for deauth, disassoc, EAPOL,
                Pwnagotchi-style beacons, Pineapple-ish gear, Flock signals and
                OpenDroneID / RemoteID drone fingerprints.
              

              

                Deauth
                EAPOL
                Pwnagotchi
                Drones
              

            


            

              

                
              

              
Heartbeat tracking

              

                Open a threat and follow the live RSSI like a weird little
                treasure hunt. The filtering keeps the beeps and sprite from
                getting too jumpy while you move around.
              

              

                Live RSSI
                Proximity
              

            


            

              

                
              

              
Wardrive mode

              

                Walk, ride or drive around and log nearby networks to Wigle 1.6
                CSVs under /valkyrie/wardrive/. It can use onboard,

Logging, alerts & control

              

                Threats go into /valkyrie/threats.log, the watch

                notified. You can toggle BLE, Wi-Fi, threat types and ignored
                devices from the watch.
